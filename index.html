05 The Shift

Communication demand slips as AI skills climb.

Each line = the % of that month's skilled postings mentioning the skill (3-mo rolling avg). Showing share controls for overall hiring volume — so this is proportion within jobs, not raw market size.

AI-related skills rising ↑ while Communication's share eases ↓
Reading the trend

Communication eases from ~57% to ~45% of postings. AI / ML skills climb off a low base.

Caveat for the room: there is a fixed set of soft skills but an ever-growing list of hard skills. Some hard-skill rise reflects new tools entering the panel — not only deeper demand per job.

10 What It Means

Widening the aperture: human-centric skills are the early-career on-ramp.

①  Demand is human-first

Soft skills are two-thirds of all demand and near-universal across every degree level — the common language of work.

②  Tech is the fast lane

Hard skills grow 1.16× faster and cluster in senior roles — but part of that rise is an ever-expanding skill list, not only deeper per-job demand.

③  The pairing pays

Hard + Soft together earn the highest salaries (+7% over soft-only). Complementary skills, not either/or.

④  Widen the search

Early careers open through human-centric skills + experience. Hiring on these widens the aperture for emerging talent.
